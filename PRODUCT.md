# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Self-motivated data scientists, ML engineers, and students who browse courses and track their own progress through lessons. They use this site independently — no instructor, no due dates, no hand-holding. Typical session: open a category, scan lesson progress, pick up where they left off, read the next lesson.

## Product Purpose

A statically-rendered course catalog and lesson browser. Users navigate a hierarchy of courses → categories → subsections → lessons, with read-status tracking that persists across sessions. The goal is to make self-guided learning feel organized, progress feel visible, and navigation feel effortless — like a well-structured syllabus that never gets lost.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, calm, capable. The tone is quietly confident — no hype, no gamification. The interface is a companion for focused learning: present when needed, invisible when not. 3-word personality: **friendly, calm, readable**.

## Anti-references

Not corporate or sterile. No sharp/jarring lines, no aggressive gradients, no SaaS-dashboard cliches (Vercel, Linear, Stripe-aesthetic). Nothing that competes with the content for attention. Avoid the hero-metric template (big number + small label) and glassmorphism-as-default.

## Design Principles

1. **Progress is the compass** — Every screen answers "where am I, what's next, what's done?" without asking. Dots, checkmarks, and order badges are the primary navigation interface, not decoration.

2. **Gentle over sharp** — Nothing startles. Soft edges, smooth transitions, quiet colors. The interface recedes so the content leads. No corporate sterility, no jarring lines.

3. **Readability is non-negotiable** — Typography, contrast, and spacing always serve the reading experience first. The user is here to learn, not to admire the UI.

4. **Frictionless flow** — The path from home to lesson feels inevitable. Reduce unnecessary choices, make the next action obvious, stay out of the learner's way.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keyboard navigation throughout. Dark mode default with sufficient contrast ratios for comfortable extended reading (all animations play freely — no reduced-motion restrictions). No reliance on color alone to convey status (order badges, checkmarks, and text labels supplement dots).
