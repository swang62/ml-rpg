# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Self-motivated data scientists, ML engineers, and students who browse courses and track their own progress by 'playing'. They use this site independently — no instructor, no due dates, no hand-holding. Typical session: open a category, scan lesson progress, pick up where they left off, read the next lesson, complete objectives/quests.

## Product Purpose

A course catalog and gamified lesson learning site. Users navigate a hierarchy of courses → categories → subsections → lessons, which is displayed as worlds -> levels -> quests -> objectives. With a player HUD and XP/lesson tracking that persists across sessions. The goal is to make self-guided learning feel fun, progress feel visible, and navigation feel effortless — like a RPG video game that never gets boring.

## Brand Personality

Fun, retro 8bit video gaming, site is like a video game console. The tone is bold, fun, exciting. The interface is a companion for fun learning: present when needed, immediate feedback when lessons are completed. Dopamine rush is the key here.

## Anti-references

Not corporate or sterile. No sharp/jarring lines, no aggressive gradients, no SaaS-dashboard cliches (Vercel, Linear, Stripe-aesthetic). Nothing that competes with the content for attention. Avoid the hero-metric template (big number + small label) and glassmorphism-as-default.

## Design Principles

1. **Progress is the compass** — Every screen answers "where am I, what's next, what's done?" without asking.

2. **Readability is non-negotiable** — Typography, contrast, and spacing always serve the reading experience first. The user is here to learn, have fun, and enjoy the ride.

3. **Frictionless flow** — The path from home to lesson feels inevitable. Reduce unnecessary choices, make the next action obvious, make all necessary info visible and rewarding.
